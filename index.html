Hi im a house music enthusiast. Heres my collection of loops and one-shots too esoteric for soundcloud.

Top 5 - 2018

5. Loods - Every Time [Shall Not Fade]


London-based Loods steps up on the '3 years of service' VA on SNF, one of the pioneers of new-wave lo-fi house. 'Every Time' is a warm, dusty lo-fi banger sampling Ill Al Skratch's 90's hip-hop classic 'Where My Homiez?'.

4. Nachtbraker - Randy [Quartet Series]


The enigma that is Maurtis Verwoerd aka Nachtbraker makes his debut on his own Quartet Series imprint, with a monolith of a release in 'When You Find a Stranger in the Alps'. The EP is filled with a collection of cuts spanning genres, from Disco edits, to ambient and jungle. 'Randy' is a quintessential Nachtbraker track, morphing from a tropical disco track to a funky Motown flavoured jam. The track, and even more so the EP, is best described through the self produced album teaser video.

3. Frits Wentink - Theme 09 [Wolf Music]


Part of the 'Two Bar House Music & Chord Stuff' series on Wolf, the Dutch wizard shows a prime example of his unique quirky style in 'Theme 09'. Dusty, detuned, tremolo Rhodes chords play out along a shuffled UKG beat, coupled with off-centre with RnB vocal snippets.

2. Demuja - Kensai [MUJA]


An insta-classic from the established Austrian. Part of his 'Classic Warrior' EP, Demuja pays homage to the classic sounds of 90's house. A solid open 909 hat-based beat, coupled with an atmospheric, almost ethereal, DX7 pad, takes you right back to a downtown Chicago joint in the early 90's. Throw in chopped-up and spoken word RnB vocals and a weighty 303-esque sub-bass, and you have possibly the biggest club track of 2018.

1. Jon Gravy - U Got It [Blaq Numbers]


Vienna-based artist Jon Gravy conjures up yet another heart-filled disco anthem in 'U Got It' on the 'Blaq Bunch Vol. 2' VA. The textbook Jon Gravy sound of open, shuffled 909 hats and unadultered booming kicks is blended with a catchy funk disco loop, and some hearty George Michael vocal samples. Definitely unlike most run-of-the-mill disco edits being spewed out on SC right now, and an absolute weapon of a track for any setting.
